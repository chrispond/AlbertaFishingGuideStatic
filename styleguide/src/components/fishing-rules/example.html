Provincial Fishing

Is made up of three main land zones; Northern Boreal, Eastern Slopes & Prairie Parkland which cover most of the province. The provincial government has created rules and regulations for all bodies of water that are within these zones. For the official detailed explanation of the Alberta fishing rules and regulations, download the 2020/2021 Alberta Guide to Sport Fishing Regulations. or visit www.albertaregulations.ca

National Park Fishing

Consists of five national parks; Banff, Jasper, Waterton Lakes, Wood Buffalo and Elk Island. Of those five only three offer good opportunity for angling. Elk Island has prohibited fishing throughout the park, and Wood Buffalo has less then ideal angler access and conditions for healthy fish populations; with the exception of the major river systems. Each national park issues their own license, and fishing regulations which are not transferable between parks. You provincial license is not recognized inside any national park.
